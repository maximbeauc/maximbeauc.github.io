About Me

Hi, I'm Maxim.

I'm a first-year Mechatronics Engineering student at the University of Waterloo. At school, I love hanging out with friends, and honestly, I love doing schoolwork. However, my favourite night of the week is Monday night at poker club.

I'm most passionate about learning new things. While software development and mechanical design are currently my main fascination, I'm eager to explore anything math and engineering related. Math was my favourite subject in elementary and high school, and I've always been fascinated by the logical thinking required in engineering.

My programming journey started in elementary school, where the block-coding Scratch peaked my interests. Later in high school, I built games in Java for my computer science courses and served as the Executive of the Computer Science Club. I've since expanded into Python, C/C++, C#, and web technologies.

In my free time, I enjoy practicing karate, where I earned a 2nd Degree Black Belt, and teaching as a karate instructor — which has taught me discipline, leadership, and how to work with others effectively.

I completed my first co-op as a Software Developer Student at Innovative Automation Inc.. Even though I was a software developer, I still gained lots of exposure to relevant engineering concepts through talking with experienced professionals both on work-related continuous projects, and in one-on-one interactions.

I'm fluent in both English and French, and I'm actively looking for my next co-op or internship opportunity.
