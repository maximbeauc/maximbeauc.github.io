Autonomous Tic-Tac-Toe Robot

Designed and built a fully autonomous robot that plays Tic-Tac-Toe against a human opponent — with a 0% loss rate. The project spanned the full engineering pipeline: mechanical design in SolidWorks, 3D-printed fabrication of custom components, motor and sensor integration, and a C++ decision algorithm that determines the optimal move for any board state.

  • Designed and modeled robotic components in SolidWorks and fabricated parts using 3D printing.
  • Integrated colour sensors and motor control systems to autonomously detect board state and place pieces.
  • Implemented C++ control logic enabling the robot to play against human opponents with a 0% loss rate.
